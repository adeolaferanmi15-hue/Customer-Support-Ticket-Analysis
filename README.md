# Customer Support Ticket Analysis Dashboard

## Project Story

Every customer complaint tells a story.

This project goes beyond numbers to uncover patterns in customer dissatisfaction, support inefficiencies, and operational gaps. The goal was to transform raw support ticket data into meaningful insights that can improve customer experience and business performance.

---

## Project Objectives

- Identify customer complaint patterns  
- Determine the most frequent issue types  
- Evaluate support agent performance  
- Measure resolution efficiency  
- Detect high-risk customers (churn risk)  
- Provide actionable, data-driven recommendations

## Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

- Total Tickets: 122  
- Total Customers: 41  
- Total Departments: 3  
- Average Resolution Time: 34 hours  

---

## Key Insights

- Bug Reports are the most common issue type  
- Low priority tickets make up the largest share and take the longest time to resolve  
- A small group of customers contributes a high number of complaints (potential churn risk)  
- John handled the highest number of tickets, indicating workload imbalance  
- The Billing department receives the most complaints  
- 34 open tickets indicate a backlog affecting service efficiency  

---

## Business Impact

This analysis highlights gaps in:

- Customer experience  
- Support efficiency  
- Workload distribution  

Addressing these issues can help:

- Reduce repeated complaints  
- Improve resolution time  
- Increase customer satisfaction  
- Minimize customer churn

## Recommendations

- Address recurring issues at the root cause  
- Improve system reliability by fixing bugs and delays  
- Balance workload across support agents  
- Introduce departmental specialization  
- Standardize ticket status definitions (Resolved vs Closed)  
- Proactively engage high-risk customers
